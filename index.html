THE SIEGE OF MARAWI CITY


What happened in Marawi City?

Marawi, a predominantly Muslim city of 200,000 people, begins to face the hard challenges of rehabilitating a battle area ravaged by the war and addressing the radical ideology that allowed the local armed groups to recruit members among residents. Marawi, situated on the southern island of Mindanao in the Philippines, was the site of a bloody urban battle between ISIL fighters and government forces.Officially known as the Islamic City of Marawi, it is the largest. Muslim city in the predominantly Catholicnation. ISIL fighters laid siege to the city in May, prompting months of heavy combat that prompted hundreds of thousands to flee and left more than 1,000 dead.
